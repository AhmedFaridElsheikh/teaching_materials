Power Consumption
Analyzer

Background

In electrical power systems, the instantaneous power consumed by a load is given by:

P = V × I     (Watts)
E = P × t     (Wh, where t = 1 hour)

Recording voltage and current every hour over a full day allows engineers to profile the daily energy consumption of a system. This task asks you to automate that analysis in Java.

Requirements

  1. Write a static method with the following signature that takes voltage and current as parameters and returns the computed power:
    public static double calculatePower(double voltage, double current)
  2. In main, declare three arrays of size 24 to store the voltage readings, current readings, and computed power values for each hour of the day.
  3. Use a loop to read voltage and current from the user for all 24 hours. Call calculatePower to populate the power array.
  4. After all input is collected, compute and print a full hourly report showing voltage, current, and power for each hour. Any hour where power exceeds 1000 W must be flagged as High Consumption.
  5. Compute and print a summary that includes: total energy consumed over 24 hours (Wh), average power (W), the peak hour and its power value, and the total count of high-consumption hours.

Expected Output Format

--- Hourly Power Report --- Hour 1 : V = 220.0 V I = 3.5 A P = 770.0 W Hour 2 : V = 220.0 V I = 5.2 A P = 1144.0 W << High Consumption Hour 3 : V = 215.0 V I = 2.1 A P = 451.5 W . . . ------------------------------------------- Total Energy Consumed : 18500.0 Wh Average Power : 770.8 W Peak Hour : Hour 2 — 1144.0 W High Consumption Hours : 6 hour(s)
